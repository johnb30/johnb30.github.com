About

Nov 24th, 2012

My name is John Beieler. I am currently a graduate student at Pennsylvania State University in Political Science. My research focuses on international conflict and applications of computational methods to problems in the social sciences. Please have a look around the site to learn more about me, my research interests, contained on the research page, and the research I have conducted, seen on the papers page.

If you have any questions, please feel free to contact me at jub270 [at] psu [dot] edu.]]

Research

My current interests focus on the applications of social network analysis, natural language processing, and machine learning to problems in the social sciences. Specifically, I am interested in examining how these types of methods can enable better predictions of behavior within the international system, such as instances of conflict. In addition, I hope to examine how emergent properties of social networks can better aid understanding of the dynamic processes that underly international interactions.

My previous research focused on applications of political psychology to international conflict and cooperation.
